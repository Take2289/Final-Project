How to Build a Bank

Money management is a critical life skill needed to succeed in life. Learning to manage your money is a crucial part of being able to live the lifestyle that you want. A budget should be made each month to distribute your income among all your expenses. By tracking your expenses you will be able to know here each dollar you earn goes. Better budgeting starts with knowing exactly where your money is going and what you have left over. Our money management calculator is a simple tool that allows you budget better by making the most out of your income.

Step 1: Enter your net month/year income. Your net pay: Amount on your paycheck minus federal taxes, Social Security, Medicare, health insurance, other benefits, etc. You can also include any other sources of income in this section.

Step 2: Start off by naming all your monthly expenses such as Housing and utilities, transportation, food and groceries, student loan payments, health and beauty, miscellaneous expenses. Enter your total monthly expenses.

Step 3: Ready to see your results! click calculate.

Finished: Total potential savings are displayed.

Advice And Tips

This calculator is provided to help you prepare for the future by examining your current financial situation thoroughly. We encourage our customers to follow the 50/30/20 rule.

50: No more than 50% of your income should be allotted for necessities such as rent, utilities, etc.

30: 30% can be used for discretionary items such as luxury items, vacations, etc.

20: We advise that at least 20% of your income should go towards savings. Of course any more than 20% is better but less is definitely not recommended.
